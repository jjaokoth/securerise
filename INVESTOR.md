# Securerise Investor Brief

## One-line pitch

A Universal Trust Layer that uses AI Vision verification and escrow handshakes to eliminate fraud in Kenyan physical asset trades across MPESA, bank, and USDC rails.

## Problem

- Kenyan informal markets suffer from a large trust gap: buyers pay before verifying delivery, and sellers wait for payment release.
- High-risk goods like vehicle parts, electronics, and spare components are frequently disputed or never delivered.
- Existing solutions rely on manual escrow or trust networks, which do not scale to high-volume asset trading.

## Solution

- Securerise creates a locked escrow handshake at transaction initiation.
- Sellers upload proof-of-delivery images to the AI Vision verifier.
- Funds are released only when the asset matches expected metadata and confidence thresholds.
- The system supports multi-rail payouts: MPESA, BANK, and USDC.

## Why now

- Digital payment adoption in Kenya is growing rapidly, but trust remains the key barrier for higher-value physical goods.
- AI Vision and composable payments are now mature enough to automate proof-of-delivery verification.
- Regulators and marketplaces are increasingly demanding fraud-resistant payment infrastructure.

## Market opportunity

- Targeting high-risk asset trades in Kenya, including electronics, appliance components, vehicle parts, and cross-border remittances.
- The model is extensible to other East African markets with the same payment rails and trust challenges.

## Differentiators

- AI-enabled proof-of-delivery rather than manual claims.
- Immutable handshake state ensures auditability.
- Developer-friendly, versioned API with a formal OpenAPI contract.
- Designed for integration with marketplace partners and logistics providers.

## Core traction narrative

- Build trust for buyers and sellers by making payment conditional on verified delivery.
- Reduce disputes and chargebacks through a provable release workflow.
- Increase transaction velocity by automating the payment-release decision.

- Highlight the product-market fit in Kenyan asset trading.
- Use the README and OpenAPI docs as the primary investor-facing collateral.
